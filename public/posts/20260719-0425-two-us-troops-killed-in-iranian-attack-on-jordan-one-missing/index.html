Why This Matters

Americans are dead, and we're still pretending Iran is some rational actor we can negotiate with. Two servicemen gave their lives in Jordan while defending American interests in the Middle East. One more is missing. This isn't a regional skirmish—this is direct Iranian aggression against the United States military, and it demands an unambiguous response, not another round of diplomatic hand-wringing.

The Biden-Harris administration spent years trying to appease Tehran, lifting sanctions and sending pallets of cash while Iranian proxies took shots at American troops with increasing brazenness. Now we're supposed to believe a strongly-worded statement will fix the problem? Real Americans understand a simple truth: weakness invites aggression. When you let enemies attack your soldiers without serious consequences, you get more attacks.

Our troops deserve leadership that has their back, not leadership that treats hostile regimes like misunderstood partners waiting for the right incentive to behave. These servicemen died doing their jobs. The least we owe them is a foreign policy that makes clear: attack Americans, and you will pay a price you can't afford.
