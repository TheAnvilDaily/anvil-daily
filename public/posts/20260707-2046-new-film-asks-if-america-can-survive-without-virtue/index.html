A new documentary asks whether the republic can survive if it loses the very thing the Founders believed made self-governance possible? That is, virtue.
⚡ Why This Matters

# Why This Matters

The Founders didn't mince words: a free people needs virtue or it dies. Not eventually. Not in some distant future. Now. They understood something our ruling class has forgotten—you can't have liberty without character. You can't vote your way to prosperity if your neighbors are stealing, lying, and cheating. Self-government only works when people actually govern themselves. When that vanishes, tyranny fills the void. Every single time.

We're watching this play out in real time. The institutions collapsing around us didn't break because of bad policy alone. They broke because the people running them stopped believing in anything beyond power and profit. Schools that won't tell the truth. Media that manufactures narratives. Politicians who serve themselves. Businesses that sell out their customers. This isn't a left-right thing anymore—it's a decency thing. And decency is hemorrhaging.

Here's what matters: you can't import virtue. You can't legislate it. You can't print it or email it. It has to live in actual people, in actual families, in actual communities. The question this film raises isn't academic—it's existential. If we don't recover what made America work, no amount of clever politics will save us. Start in your own house. Start with your own word meaning something. That's not preachy. That's survival.
