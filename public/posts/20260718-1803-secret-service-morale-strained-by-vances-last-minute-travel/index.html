Secret Service morale strained by Vances' last-minute travel demands, report says

Published July 18, 2026 · 06:03 PM UTC
Read full story at Washington Times →
REPORT: J.D. Vance's Secret Service detail is voicing frustration over a pattern of last-minute, costly travel requests from the vice president and his family.
Why This Matters

The Secret Service signed up to protect the VP, not to play logistics roulette every time the Vances get a travel itch. Last-minute demands mean agents can't do their jobs right—they're scrambling instead of planning, cutting corners instead of securing perimeters. That's not efficient government. That's chaos, and chaos gets people hurt.

Real Americans understand: you plan ahead, you respect the people working for you, you don't treat a security detail like your personal concierge service. These agents have families at home. They're working overtime because someone decided a trip needs to happen tomorrow instead of next week. Meanwhile, the bill hits taxpayers. Your wallet pays for the scramble.

This matters because it reveals how an administration actually operates behind closed doors. Do they respect the people underneath them? Do they understand that government works on discipline and planning, not impulse? The Vances aren't the problem—the problem is an operation that's so loose, so last-minute, that it strains the very people tasked with keeping them safe. That's not leadership. That's telling.
