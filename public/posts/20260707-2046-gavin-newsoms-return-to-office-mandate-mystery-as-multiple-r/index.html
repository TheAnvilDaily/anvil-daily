⚡ Why This Matters

Gavin Newsom just ordered state workers back to the office four days a week—but apparently the rules don't apply to everyone equally. Multiple hybrid and remote positions are still being advertised across his administration, raising the obvious question: is this mandate for thee but not for me? Working Californians have been dragged back to cubicles while paying some of the highest taxes in the nation, yet Sacramento insiders seem to enjoy flexibility that regular folks can only dream about.

This is classic government hypocrisy. When remote work helps the ruling class stay comfortable, it's suddenly indispensable. When it threatens to expose how bloated the state bureaucracy has become, suddenly we need everyone back in the office. But not everyone—just the people without connections. The selective enforcement tells you everything you need to know about who really runs California.

Real Americans are watching their neighbors get squeezed while elites write the rules for everyone else. Until Newsom applies his return-to-office mandate equally—across the board, no exceptions—this looks like what it is: political theater. Sacramento doesn't trust its own medicine, and that's a problem.
