Trump Releases Election Files Alleging Foreign Threats

Published July 18, 2026 · 06:03 PM UTC
Read full story at RealClearPolitics →
In a national address on Thursday night, President Trump declassified troves of documents that he said proved serious vulnerabilities in American elections. The White House website published four batches of disclosures that China has stolen the voting records of 220 million Americans, that voting machines are vulnerable to outside hacks, that the intelligence community downplayed risks in 2020, and that 278,000 noncitizens are registered to vote.
Why This Matters

# Why This Matters

For years, the establishment told you to stop asking questions about election security. "Move on," they said. "Nothing to see here." Now Trump's declassified documents prove what millions of Americans already knew in their gut: our elections have serious problems, and the people running them knew it. China stealing voting records of 220 million Americans isn't a conspiracy theory—it's in the government's own files. The fact that we're learning this NOW tells you everything about who was protecting whom.

Voting machines vulnerable to hacks? Noncitizens on the rolls? An intelligence community that downplayed risks when it mattered most? These aren't minor technical glitches—they're the foundation of our democracy being treated like a convenience store. Real Americans don't need a college degree to understand what this means: if we can't trust our elections, we can't trust anything. That's not partisan. That's common sense.

The question isn't whether Trump releasing these files is "appropriate" by Washington standards. The question is why it took a president willing to buck the system to get the truth out. Every voter deserves to know their vote actually counts. Every citizen deserves to know that foreign adversaries aren't walking through the front door while our own officials look the other way.
