Why This Matters

This isn't some dusty historical footnote—this is a direct warning about how elections get stolen in the modern era. Venezuela's regime didn't need tanks rolling down the street. They just needed access to the machines counting the votes. And here's the kicker: our own intelligence community has known this was possible for nearly two decades. While one side of our political debate insisted electronic voting was "safe, secure, and tested," our spies were quietly documenting how autocrats flip elections with the push of a button.

The timing of Trump declassifying this matters just as much as the content. For years, anyone who raised questions about voting machine vulnerabilities got smeared as a conspiracy theorist. Meanwhile, the CIA had documented evidence sitting in classified files that hostile regimes—actual, real, documented hostile regimes—could and did manipulate elections electronically. That's not fringe talk. That's our government's own intelligence assessment. Americans deserve to know what our own agencies knew about these vulnerabilities.

Common sense tells you what this means: secure elections require verifiable paper ballots and transparent counting. Not some black-box machines that only "experts" can audit. Not systems where election officials claim you have to "just trust us." Venezuela's playbook is a master class in how to rig an election without anyone seeing the knife. We should learn from it—and fix our system before anyone gets ideas about using those same techniques here at home.
