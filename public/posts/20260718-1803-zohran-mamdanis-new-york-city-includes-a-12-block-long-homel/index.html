Why This Matters

This is what happens when politicians treat homelessness like a virtue signal instead of a real problem. Zohran Mamdani and his progressive allies didn't build shelters or demand accountability—they built a 12-block open-air drug market and called it compassion. Meanwhile, law-abiding New Yorkers pay record taxes to step over needles on their way to work. That's not helping the homeless. That's abandoning them and your city.

Los Angeles tried this experiment. It failed spectacularly. Crime exploded. Businesses fled. Property values cratered. And somehow, New York's Democratic machine decided to copy that exact playbook anyway. Nobody asked the residents of the West Side if they wanted their neighborhood turned into a cautionary tale. Nobody asked the small business owners if this was the vision they had for their livelihoods.

Real compassion means getting people off the streets and into treatment, not enabling them to rot in a sprawling encampment while everyone else watches their city decay. New York deserves better than this—and so do the homeless people trapped in these conditions. This isn't progress. It's surrender.
