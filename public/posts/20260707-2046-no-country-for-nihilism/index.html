# No Country for Nihilism

Hollywood wants you to believe that life is meaningless, that evil wins, that decent people are just chumps waiting to get steamrolled. That's the lie peddled by the culture industry—and it's poisoning millions of Americans who've given up fighting back. Cormac McCarthy's masterpiece isn't some nihilist manifesto. It's a warning about what happens when ordinary folks stop believing in something bigger than themselves. The book demands moral clarity, not moral surrender.

The critics who twist McCarthy into a spokesman for despair are doing exactly what they always do: take our greatest works and use them as weapons against the values that built this country. Real Americans understand that darkness exists, yes—but they also understand that you don't roll over. You stand firm. You protect what matters. That's not naive. That's everything.

Stop letting the coastal elite tell you what our own stories mean. Read it yourself. Think for yourself. The answer isn't nihilism—it's the courage to believe that good people still matter, that choices still count, and that surrender is never the only option.
