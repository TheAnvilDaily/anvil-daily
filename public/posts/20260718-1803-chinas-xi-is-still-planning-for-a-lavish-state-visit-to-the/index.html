# Why This Matters

Beijing's dictator is still measuring the White House curtains while Trump's drawing a line. Xi Jinping doesn't show up for state visits—the kind with the red carpets and the pomp—unless he smells weakness. The fact that he's banking on one tells you everything you need to know about what's happening behind closed doors. Either China thinks they can charm their way past Trump's tariffs and tough talk, or they're betting the pressure campaign works and a new negotiating team caves. Neither scenario is good for American workers watching their jobs get shipped overseas.

The last president let these state visits happen on China's schedule, turning the Oval Office into a genteel negotiating disadvantage. You don't give a regime that steals your intellectual property, threatens your allies, and meddles in your elections the symbolic victory of a state dinner. Trump understands this isn't about being rude—it's about leverage. Every ceremonial gesture is a bargaining chip. Every bow to protocol is a concession you can't take back.

Real Americans didn't vote for civility with communists. They voted for someone who puts America first, which means making Beijing sweat before anyone rolls out the red carpet. If Xi wants a visit, he can get serious about trade, Taiwan, and respecting American interests. Until then, he can wait by the phone.
