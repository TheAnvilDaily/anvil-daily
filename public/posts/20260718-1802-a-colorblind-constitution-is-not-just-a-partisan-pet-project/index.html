# Why This Matters

The Left wants you to believe that treating people equally under the law is somehow a radical right-wing conspiracy. It's not. A colorblind Constitution—the idea that government shouldn't sort citizens by skin color—is the actual civil rights dream. MLK didn't march so we could divide Americans into racial categories and dole out benefits by checking boxes. He marched so we'd judge people by their character, not their color. But today's progressive establishment has inverted that vision, and they're betting most Americans won't notice.

Here's what's really happening: while DC insiders debate "colorblindness" as if it's debatable, ordinary people are watching their kids get passed over for jobs and college slots. They're seeing their neighborhoods transformed by policy decisions made in ivory towers with zero accountability. They're tired of politicians lecturing them about "systemic racism" while systemically rigging the game based on race. A colorblind Constitution isn't a pet project for conservatives—it's the last line of defense for actual equal opportunity.

The Washington Post wants you to think this is complicated. It isn't. Either we treat Americans as individuals or we don't. Either the law applies equally or it doesn't. You can't have it both ways and claim to believe in justice. Real Americans understand this instinctively, which is exactly why the ruling class works so hard to obscure it.
