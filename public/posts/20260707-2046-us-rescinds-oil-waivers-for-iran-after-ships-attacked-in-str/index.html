⚡ Why This Matters

The Biden administration finally did something right—and only after Iran literally attacked our ships. Let's be clear: these oil waivers should have never existed in the first place. We were essentially paying Iran to behave, and they repaid us by targeting American vessels in international waters. That's not diplomacy. That's appeasement with a price tag, and hardworking Americans are the ones footing the bill at the pump.

This is what happens when you lead from behind. For months, Iran has tested us—drones, missiles, proxy attacks—and we kept writing permission slips. Now we're finally pulling back the oil money spigot, but the damage is done. Every waiver we handed out was a signal that there's a price for restraint and weakness gets you a seat at the negotiating table. Real strength means consequences arrive immediately, not after you have to sink a few more ships to get our attention.

Here's what matters to your wallet: sanctioning Iran's oil exports means less supply flooding the global market, which means prices at the pump stay higher longer. But you know what? That's the cost of actually standing up to terrorists instead of bankrolling them. At least we're finally speaking a language Tehran understands.
