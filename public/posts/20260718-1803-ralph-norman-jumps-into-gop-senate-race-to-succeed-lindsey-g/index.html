Ralph Norman jumps into GOP Senate race to succeed Lindsey Graham

Published July 18, 2026 · 06:03 PM UTC
Read full story at Washington Times →
GOP Rep. Ralph Norman of South Carolina is making a run to fill longtime Sen. Lindsey Graham's seat following his death.
Why This Matters

Ralph Norman's jumping in the race to replace Lindsey Graham, and conservatives need to wake up. Graham spent decades in the Senate talking tough while cutting backroom deals—and Norman represents a chance to clean house. This is about whether South Carolina voters want another go-along-get-along establishment politician or someone who'll actually fight for their interests without apologizing to the media.

Here's the thing: Senate seats don't stay conservative on their own. They drift left when we let establishment figures run the show unchallenged. Norman's congressional record shows he's willing to stand alone when it matters. That's exactly what the Senate needs—people who remember they work for their constituents, not the cocktail circuit.

This race matters because it'll tell us whether Republicans learned anything. Do we want fighters or fundraisers? The answer should be obvious, but with Washington it never is. Norman's in. Now we find out if South Carolina voters are ready for real change.
