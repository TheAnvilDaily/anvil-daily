# Why This Matters

Law and order means something simple: you don't get to endanger lives because you're seeking attention on social media. This guy broke into a locomotive—a 200-ton machine carrying cargo down the tracks—and decided the roof was his personal stage. One slip, one miscalculation, and he's not just a headline—he's a cautionary tale. Worse, he's created a genuine hazard for train operators and emergency responders who now have to deal with the chaos.

We've normalized the idea that any stunt, no matter how reckless, is acceptable if it gets you eyeballs online. Meanwhile, working people—railroad employees, law enforcement, first responders—are cleaning up the mess. This isn't about being fun or edgy. It's about basic respect for other people's safety and their jobs.

Simple rule: your right to be noticed ends where someone else's safety begins. Arizona got this one right.
