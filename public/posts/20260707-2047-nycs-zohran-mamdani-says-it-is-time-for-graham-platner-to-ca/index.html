This is what happens when the Democrat machine decides you're not radical enough—they send in the out-of-state fixers to clean up their mess. Mamdani didn't build his political career in Maine. The real story? Graham Platner committed the cardinal sin of stepping out of line, and now his own party is turning on him like a pack of wolves. When a far-left activist from Manhattan is calling the shots in a Maine Senate race, you know the Democrat establishment has lost the plot. They don't care what regular voters want anymore. They care about control. They care about purity tests. They care about power.

Maine voters should remember this moment. Remember who tried to silence their choices. Remember which party thinks New York City knows better than Maine does. When November comes around, that's a reason to vote for anyone BUT the Democrats' preferred candidate—whoever that ends up being.
