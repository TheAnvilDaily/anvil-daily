Sheinbaum to attend World Cup final after accepting Trump's invitation

Published July 19, 2026 · 04:26 AM UTC

Mexican President Claudia Sheinbaum said she plans to attend Sunday's World Cup title game after receiving an invitation from President Trump.
Why This Matters

Mexico's president is flying to Las Vegas to watch soccer with Donald Trump while her country burns. Cartels control entire regions. People are getting kidnapped at the border. But sure, enjoy the game.

This is what happens when you have a weak leader in Mexico City. Trump extends an olive branch—a simple gesture to build goodwill—and Sheinbaum gets a photo op while her own citizens suffer under organized crime. You can't buy legitimacy with a football match.

Real Americans are watching this and seeing exactly what they suspected: Mexico's leadership doesn't have its act together. Trump knows the score. He's extending the invitation anyway because he understands leverage and diplomacy. The question is whether Sheinbaum will actually use this moment to commit to real action on the border and cartels—or if she's just there for the halftime entertainment.
