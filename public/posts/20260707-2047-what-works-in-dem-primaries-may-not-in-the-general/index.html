What Works in Dem Primaries May Not in the General

Published July 07, 2026 · 08:47 PM UTC
Read full story at RealClearPolitics →
Democratic socialists won safe seats in New York and Colorado. Michigan will test if the Democrats' far-left strategy can work in a swing state.
⚡ Why This Matters

The Democratic Party's Bernie Sanders caucus keeps winning in deep-blue strongholds where they could run a traffic cone and win. New York, Colorado—these are places where "D" next to your name is your golden ticket. But here's the reality check: winning a primary by out-lefting your opponent doesn't mean you can carry a pizza shop owner in Michigan or convince a steelworker in Pennsylvania that your agenda works for him. Primaries reward the loudest activists. General elections reward candidates who can actually govern.

Michigan is the test that matters. If the far-left socialist wing can win a swing state where people still care about paychecks and crime and their kids' schools—not just climate theory and pronouns—then Democrats have found something. If they can't, then every primary victory from Brooklyn to Boulder is just expensive practice. Middle America isn't watching MSNBC. They're watching their grocery bills and asking which party won't make things worse.

This is why Democrats keep losing heartland voters. They keep nominating candidates who crush it with the activist base and then wonder why they can't close the deal in actual competitive places. You can win a thousand safe seats and still lose the country.
