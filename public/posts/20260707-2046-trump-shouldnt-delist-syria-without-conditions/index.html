Trump Shouldn’t Delist Syria Without Conditions

Published July 07, 2026 · 08:46 PM UTC
Read full story at National Review →
The inclusion of Syria on the U.S. State Sponsors of Terrorism list is important leverage the U.S. should not give up easily.
⚡ Why This Matters

Look, we've got one card left to play with Assad, and we're supposed to just hand it over? Syria's on the terrorism list for a reason—because they've earned it through decades of sponsoring Hezbollah, harboring terrorists, and making the Middle East worse. That's real leverage. The moment we delist them without getting anything concrete back, we lose our only meaningful tool to influence their behavior. This isn't about being tough just to be tough; it's about basic negotiating sense that any businessman would understand.

The Biden-Harris crowd has been desperate to "normalize" Assad for months now, but normalizing a state sponsor of terrorism sends exactly the wrong signal to every other bad actor watching. It tells Iran, Russia, and every terrorist outfit that if you wait long enough and play nice enough, America will fold first. Our allies in the region—Israel, the Gulf states, Jordan—are watching to see if our word means anything. Delisting Syria with no strings attached tells them it doesn't.

Here's the bottom line: leverage is worthless if you give it away. Any deal worth doing requires real concessions—on terrorism, on human rights, on narcotics trafficking, on Iranian influence. Trump's instinct to negotiate is sound. But negotiate from strength, not weakness. Keep Syria on that list until they actually change their behavior. That's not isolationism; that's just common sense.
