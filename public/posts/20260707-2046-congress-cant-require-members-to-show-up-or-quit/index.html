Members of Congress who wander away from the job face no immediate consequences, holding the seat long after they lose their ability to work.
⚡ Why This Matters

# Why This Matters

Your boss would fire you if you stopped showing up. Your doctor's license gets revoked if you ghost your patients. But Members of Congress? They can vanish from the job for months while keeping their six-figure salary, their taxpayer-funded benefits, and their seat. It's a racket that would get anyone else in America canned by lunch. These aren't conscientious objectors—they're deadweight living off you while doing nothing. The rules let them stick around like rust on a bumper until their term ends, consequences be damned.

This is why normal Americans are sick of Washington. A representative who doesn't show up to represent you isn't a representative—they're a tourist with security clearance. Yet Congress refuses to police its own, refuses to demand basic accountability, refuses to do the obvious thing and make members actually show up or resign. Instead, they protect the gig, every single one of them, because they're all counting on the same protection when they decide to check out.

Until Congress makes attendance mandatory with real teeth—removal, censure, pay docked, whatever it takes—nothing changes. The people fund this machine. They deserve someone actually sitting in those seats, not a phantom collecting a paycheck. It's not complicated. Make them work or make them leave.
