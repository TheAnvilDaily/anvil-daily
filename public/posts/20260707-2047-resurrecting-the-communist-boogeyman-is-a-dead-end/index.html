# Resurrecting the Communist Boogeyman Is a Dead End

Look, we get it. The Biden crowd is scared. So scared they're dusting off Cold War rhetoric that put people to sleep in 1985. "Communism this, socialism that"—meanwhile, working Americans are worried about affording groceries and rent *today*, not about Lenin statues that don't exist. It's the oldest trick in the book: distract from real problems with monsters in the closet. But Americans aren't buying it anymore because they're too busy paying their bills.

The presidency is supposed to be about solving problems, not running a history seminar. Families don't care about your ideological boogeyman when their paychecks don't stretch to Friday. Crime is real. The border is a mess. Inflation gutted savings accounts. These aren't abstract political concepts—they're the grinding reality of everyday life. Screaming "communist!" at every policy complaint doesn't lower gas prices or stop fentanyl from flooding our streets.

Here's what matters: messaging divorced from results is just noise. You can invoke every 20th-century nightmare, but if your policies aren't fixing what's broken, voters will move on to someone who actually has a plan. Americans respond to strength and solutions, not desperation and red-scare reruns. The narrative game is over. Deliver results, or get replaced.
