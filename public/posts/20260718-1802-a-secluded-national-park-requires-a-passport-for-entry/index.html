Why This Matters

Your own government just turned a national park into a gated community—and most Americans don't even know it yet. The National Park of American Samoa now demands you show your papers just to walk around what's supposed to be public land. A passport or birth certificate to see trees and beaches owned by every American taxpayer? That's not conservation—that's bureaucratic gatekeeping dressed up as policy.

Here's the real kicker: while Washington lecture us about "access" and "equity," they're literally locking everyday families out of their own national parks unless they've got documents that cost time and money to obtain. It's the same crowd that calls border enforcement "divisive" but has zero problem creating their own internal checkpoints. Rules for thee, but not for me.

This is what happens when unelected bureaucrats think they know better than Americans. You paid for that park. Your taxes built it. And now you need permission slips just to visit. Welcome to 2024, where the government owns your property—you just get to ask nicely if you can see it.
